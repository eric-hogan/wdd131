What do all the numbers mean?

In disc golf, most discs are labeled with four numbers that describe how they’re designed to fly. These are called flight ratings, and they help players choose the right disc for different types of throws. Here’s what each number means:

Speed (1–15):

This tells you how fast the disc needs to be thrown to perform as intended. Higher numbers (like 12–14) are for fast, powerful throws—usually drivers. Lower numbers (1–3) are slower and easier to control—like putters.

Glide (1–7):

Glide measures how long a disc stays in the air. A higher glide rating means the disc will float farther, which is great for beginners or long approaches. Lower glide is better for controlled, short-range shots.

Turn (-5 to +1):

This shows how much the disc will curve to the right (for a right-hand backhand throw) during the early part of its flight. A more negative number means more turn, which can help with distance or anhyzer shots.

Fade (0–5):

Fade describes how sharply the disc will hook left at the end of its flight (again, for a right-hand backhand throw). A higher fade means a stronger finish, while a lower fade means a straighter landing.
